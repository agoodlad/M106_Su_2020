Impossibility Theorems With two alternative votings sytems, there is basically one unique "fair" voting system, i.e. plurality. When there three or more alternatives, not only is it hard to comes up with a good voting system, it's literally IMPOSSIBLE to come up with a voting system that has EVERY desired property: Arrow's Impossibility Theorem (Thm. 11.1) The only ordinal voting system with at least three alternatives that satisifes weak pareto and IIA is a dictatorship. Gibbard's Theorem (Thm. 11.2) The only system at least three alternatives which satisfies the unanimity and strategy proof fairness principles is a dictatorship.
